Lesson 2:

Intro

In this lesson we will focus on how to read your cards. You will learn what the different parts of your card mean and how to play them. Before we dive in let’s clarify what a spell is. In Magic there are a set of cards that are lands. These cards are not considered spells and can not be interacted with. Below is an example of a land. A card is a land if it has the type “Land”. Card types are in the center of the card.

Card Costs

A card is split into many different sections. We will begin at the top and work our way down, using the card below as a reference. In the top left of the card is the card name. In the top right is the mana cost. We use lands to pay for spells. To use a land we would “tap” it or turn it sideways. On our example card we see 2 “sun” or “white” symbols which indicates we need to use 2 plains cards for this card. We also see a gray circle with a number 3 inside. This tells us we need to use 3 other lands in addition to the 2 white ones to pay for this spell. This is called generic mana and can be any color and does not have to be 3 of the same color.

Card Types

Moving past the card art we arrive at the lower section of the card. This section contains all the information about the card. The first set of information is the card type. There are 7 different card types. There are Creatures, artifacts, enchantments, instants, sorcery, and Plainswalkers. Since ours is a creature it has an additional type “Angel” saying what type of creature it is. Under that we arrive at the text box. This is where we will find “keywords” which are abilities the creature has. Our creature has Flying and Vigilance which means it can only be blocked by flying creatures and does not tap when it attacks like other creatures. At the bottom we see the power toughness. The power is how much damage the creature does and the toughness is how much health they have.
